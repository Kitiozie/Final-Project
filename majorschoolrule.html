Shiking

Be where you are supposed to be!

Ways to prevent shiking:

  1. Signing out in the USO before leaving campus/top of campus.
  2. Check in on dorm and be back on dorm on time.
  3. Attend classes and events (Community collection, Meeting for worship, Class collections and etc...).
  4. Dorms are built for separate genders. No girls should be on boys dorm and no boys should be on girls dorm, unless if you are seeing a specific teacher during study hall.
  5. Please DO NOT do the following:
    • Walking on campus at midnight
    • Driving to Trader Joes and Burger King during class time

P.S. Did you know that the cabin does not count as the top of campus?

Academic Honesty

Remember to always cite your sources!

"While academic dishonesty from one discipline to another may look different—i.e. not citing sources in English to using a generative AI to write code in a computer science course—a student's use of any work not their own is academic dishonesty" (Upper School Handbook 42).

Please:

  • No cheating
  • No helping others to cheat
  • No use of AI unless specified
  • No plagiarising
  • No fabricating data or information

P.S Did you know that students would recieve similar consequences if they aid others to cheat?

Automobile Usage

Automobiles:

  1. Student may ride in cars only with pre-approved adults (Staff, parents, pr a parent-named person).
  2. Even with parental approval, non-Westtown drivers need extra approval from dean.
  3. Boarding students generally can't have cars on campus.
  4. Day students can only use cars to commute to/from school
  5. Cars must be parked in the East Lot, permits displayed, and keys turned in to the office.
  6. No ride sharing without dean approval.
  7. No hitchhiking (Asking for free rides from passing strangers), motorcycles or scooters on campus.
  8. No ridesharing without dean approval.
  9. Speed Limit: 15 mph

Bicycles & Skateboarding:

  1. Helmets are required for bikng (on/off campus).
  2. Bikes must be locked to racks or stored in designated areas only.
  3. Skateboarding/Rollerblading on campus needs prior dean approval and helmet use.

Violations result in loss of skate/ride privileges.

Misusing car privileges could take away student's car access and lead to disciplinary action.

Bullying and Others

"Behavior that constitutes bullying, harassment, discrimination, hazing, sexual assault, and sexual harassment are considered major school rules and will not be tolerated"(Upper School Handbook 48).

  1. Bullying:
    • Repeated, intentional behavior meant to hurt, scare, or harm someone, either physically or emotionally
    • Targeting people because of their race, gender, appearance, or other personal traits
  2. Harassment:
    • Any unwanted comment or action that creates a hostile or uncomfortable environment for someone.
    • It can be verbal, written, or physical, and it makes the target feel unsafe or disrespected.
  3. Discrimination:
    • Treating someone unfairly based on a personal characteristic rather than on their actions/character.
    • Personal characteristic includes race, gender, religion, or sexual orientation.
    • It can happen in school, activities, or daily interactions.
  4. Hazing:
    • Forcing or pressuring someone to do dangerous, embarrassing, or harmful things to join or stay in a group, team, or club.
    • Even if the person agrees to it, it's still against the school rules.
  5. Sexual Assault:
    • Any sexual contact or activity that happens without clear, willing and ongoing consent from both people.
    • Consent must be freely given, not forces, pressured, or given when someone is unable to make decisions.
  6. Sexual Harassment:
    • Includes unwanted sexual comments, advances, gestures, or gender-based behavior that makes someone feel uncomfortable, intimidated, or unsafe.
    • It can also include sexual jokes, suggestive remarks, or requests for sexual favors.

Prohibited Behaviors:

  • Possessing, using or distributing illegal drugs, controlled substances, or alcohol.

  • Returning to school with evidence of substance use/misuse
  • Selling or distributing prohibited substance to peers.

Others

Theft, Destruction, and Open Flame

Theft:

  1. No stealing
  2. No attempt to steal
  3. No possession of stolen property
  4. No possession of school keys/key cards to enter areas you don't have permission to access.
  5. No accessing other's emails, mail, or accounts without their consent.
  6. No impersonating someone else or creating fake accounts (for school/social media).

Destruction, Defacement and Abuse:

  1. No defacement of personal/school property.
  2. No destruction of personal/school property.
  3. No abuse of personal/school property.

(This includes messing with fire alarms and fire extinguishers).

Open Flame:

  1. No fire-based items allowed inside or near any school building.

(This includes matches, lighters, candles, incense, or cigarettes).

P.S. Violating Open Flame policy results in automatic dismissal.
